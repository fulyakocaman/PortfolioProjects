Prediabetes


Prediabetes is a serious health condition where blood sugar levels are higher than normal, but not high enough yet to be diagnosed as type 2 diabetes. Approximately 96 million American adults—more than 1 in 3—have prediabetes. Of those with prediabetes, more than 80% don't know they have it.

Getting diagnosed with prediabetes is a serious wake-up call, but it doesn't have to mean you will definitely get diabetes. There is still time to turn things around. It’s an opportunity to initiate lifestyle changes or treatments, and potentially retard progression to diabetes or even prevent diabetes. Making these changes in your daily habits is a good way to start:

1. Become more active

2. Lower your weight

3. Make sleep a priority

4. See your doctor often

5. Eat better

6. Choose and commit to healthy lifestyle
